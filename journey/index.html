My Journey

Seven chapters.

A village in rural Bengal to global engineering leadership. Same person; many rooms.

  1. 01

    Childhood

    Village Roots

    Bankura · West Bengal · India

    Born and raised in a remote village in rural Bengal. Days were simple: tube-well water, fields, the company of grandparents, and a school that fit in two classrooms. I learned, very early, that constraints make you resourceful and that family is the first form of safety net.

    • Simplicity
    • Gratitude
    • Resilience
    • Hard work
    • Family values
  2. 02

    Age 9–13

    Residential School

    Satyanarayan Academy, Bankura

    Living away from home before I knew how to make my own bed. The dormitory taught me what no parent can — how to depend on yourself, how to make friends with people very different from you, and how to be okay alone. Resilience as a daily skill, not an idea.

    • Self-dependence
    • Responsibility
    • Adaptability
    • Discipline
  3. 03

    Adolescence

    Expanding Horizons

    Chinmaya Vidyalaya, Bokaro

    A school that took character as seriously as marks. Daily mantras, a structured routine, and teachers who treated us like adults-in-training. The first time I felt the difference between being smart and being formed.

    • Character
    • Discipline
    • Broader thinking
    • Personal growth
  4. 04

    College

    Engineering Foundations

    Asansol Engineering College

    Four years of learning to think in systems. Code was a side-effect; the real lesson was problem decomposition — taking a fuzzy ask, drawing the right boxes, and choosing what to build first. I still use that muscle every day.

    • Technology
    • Problem solving
    • Systems thinking
  5. 05

    First job

    Professional Foundation

    TCS

    My first taste of enterprise scale and what professionalism looks like in practice — being on time, owning your commitments, communicating cleanly with people you barely know. The unglamorous fundamentals I still teach to first-time managers.

    • Professionalism
    • Enterprise tech
    • Delivery discipline
  6. 06

    Mid-career

    Global Technology Journey

    Yahoo · Samsung · Microsoft · Salesforce

    Four very different companies, four very different lessons. Yahoo taught me what scale really means when a service has hundreds of millions of users. Samsung taught me product thinking — the device on the shelf is the only thing that matters. Microsoft taught me engineering excellence; the standards bar there reset what I thought "good" was. Salesforce is where leadership, mentorship, and platform thinking came together — and where most of the work on the Professional page happened.

    • Scale
    • Product thinking
    • Engineering excellence
    • Leadership
    • Mentoring
  7. 07

    Now

    Future Evolution

    Engineer → Leader → Mentor → Coach → Guide

    The arc I notice in myself is from <em>doing the work</em> to <em>helping others do their best work</em>, and now to <em>helping people see their own lives more clearly</em>. This site is part of that next chapter — personal finance, career coaching, leadership development, and a small community where we figure things out together.

    • Personal finance
    • Career coaching
    • Leadership development
    • Community building

What ties them together

A pattern I only saw later.

Each chapter took something away — proximity, comfort, certainty — and gave back a different kind of strength. The village gave me roots; residential school gave me independence; college gave me method; the global tech years gave me scale and craft. None of them, on their own, made the person.

What I carry now is the through-line: work hard, stay curious, treat people with respect, keep showing up, and tell the truth as you see it. Everything else is a footnote.
