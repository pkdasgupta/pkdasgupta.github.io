Community

A small, slow community.

I do not run a course. I do not sell anything. The ways to stay in touch are deliberately few — a monthly letter, a small handful of conversations, the doorway of the contact form. The plan is to keep it small enough that it remains real.

The Monthly Letter

One email a month. The new essay, three things I am thinking about, two books worth knowing about, one question for you to sit with. Sent on the last Sunday of the month. Skipped during travel without apology.

No tracking. No upsells. Unsubscribe is one click and I will not chase you.

Mentorship Circles

Twice a year I run a small structured cohort — six to eight people, six weeks, one of the three lenses (finance, career, leadership). It is invitation-driven. If you would like to be considered, write to me with the lens you are interested in and a paragraph on what you are trying to figure out.

Express interest

One-to-One

I take a small number of one-to-one mentorship conversations every quarter. They are free; the cost is honesty and follow-through. The best route in is the contact form below the relevant lens — it tells me what you are trying to work out and helps me be useful from the first conversation.

Elsewhere

I am quiet on most platforms by design. The two places to find me, if you want to follow loosely:

  • LinkedIn — long-form professional notes, a few times a month.
  • Right here — the slow essays, the only place I am writing at length.

There is no Twitter, no Instagram, no Substack mirror, no Telegram channel — by choice. The home is here. Thank you for visiting it slowly.
